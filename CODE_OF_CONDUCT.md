# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming, inclusive, and respectful environment for all contributors, users, and community members of the OpenClaw project. We believe that diverse perspectives and inclusive collaboration strengthen our project and community.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to creating a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful**: Treating all community members with courtesy and professionalism
- **Assuming good intent**: Interpreting others' communications charitably and offering clarification when misunderstandings arise
- **Constructive feedback**: Providing thoughtful, actionable suggestions that focus on ideas rather than individuals
- **Active listening**: Genuinely considering different viewpoints and experiences
- **Inclusivity**: Welcoming participation from people of all backgrounds and experience levels
- **Collaborative problem-solving**: Working together to resolve disagreements constructively
- **Acknowledging mistakes**: Taking responsibility for errors and using them as learning opportunities
- **Respecting boundaries**: Honoring others' time, privacy, and stated preferences

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Derogatory language or unwelcoming comments related to personal characteristics, experience level, or affiliations
- Sustained disruption of discussions or activities
- Threats, intimidation, or coercive behavior
- Persistent unwelcome attention despite clear requests to stop
- Sharing others' private information without consent
- Deliberate disruption of collaboration or technical work
- Any conduct that would reasonably make others feel unsafe or unwelcome

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for:

- Clarifying and enforcing our standards of conduct
- Taking appropriate action in response to unacceptable behavior
- Ensuring that our community remains welcoming and productive
- Addressing concerns with fairness and transparency

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project spaces, including repositories, issue trackers, discussions, and documentation
- Official project communications and events
- Informal spaces where community members identify as representing the project
- Community members' conduct when representing the project in public spaces

## Enforcement

Community members who observe behavior that violates this Code of Conduct are encouraged to report it. Reports will be handled with discretion and respect for the privacy of all parties involved.

### Reporting

To report a concern, please contact the project maintainers at: **hello@<your-domain>**

When reporting, please include:

- A clear description of the concern
- Specific examples or evidence, if available
- Any relevant context about the situation
- Your contact information (so we can follow up if needed)

### Response Process

Upon receiving a report, the project team will:

1. Acknowledge receipt of the report promptly
2. Investigate the concern thoroughly and impartially
3. Consider the full context and perspectives of those involved
4. Communicate with relevant parties transparently
5. Take appropriate action proportionate to the situation

Possible responses may include:

- Clarification and dialogue with the community member
- Request for adjustment of behavior going forward
- Temporary restrictions on participation
- Permanent removal from the project community if necessary
