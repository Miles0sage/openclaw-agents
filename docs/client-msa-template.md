# Master Service Agreement (MSA)

**Effective Date:** [DATE]

## 1. DEFINITIONS

**"Agency"** means [COMPANY NAME], a single-member LLC, and its authorized representatives.

**"Client"** means the entity identified in the Statement of Work (SOW) and its authorized representatives.

**"Deliverables"** means the tangible work product(s) created by Agency under this Agreement, including but not limited to software code, automation scripts, documentation, and consulting reports.

**"Services"** means the AI software development, automation, and consulting services described in an executed Statement of Work (SOW).

**"AI Models"** means third-party artificial intelligence systems, including but not limited to OpenAI's GPT models, Anthropic's Claude, Google's Gemini, and other publicly available or proprietary AI systems.

**"Confidential Information"** means all non-public information disclosed by either party, including client data, business plans, technical information, and AI model configurations.

**"Intellectual Property" or "IP"** means all patents, copyrights, trademarks, trade secrets, and other intellectual property rights.

**"Statement of Work" or "SOW"** means a document executed by both parties that specifies the scope, timeline, deliverables, and fees for specific Services.

## 2. SCOPE OF SERVICES

### 2.1 Service Categories

Agency provides specialized services in the following areas:

**(a) AI Software Development**

- Building custom applications powered by AI models
- Integration of AI capabilities into existing systems
- Machine learning model evaluation and implementation
- RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) systems and knowledge management tools
- Full-stack development with AI components

**(b) Automation**

- Workflow automation and process optimization
- Integration between systems using APIs and webhooks
- Data processing and transformation pipelines
- Repetitive task automation using code and AI

**(c) Consulting**

- AI capability assessment and feasibility studies
- Technical architecture and implementation strategy
- Best practices guidance and recommendations
- Training and knowledge transfer sessions

### 2.2 Engagement Model

Each engagement is defined by a separate Statement of Work (SOW) that specifies:

- Detailed scope of Services
- Deliverables and acceptance criteria
- Timeline and milestones
- Fees and payment schedule
- Any special terms or conditions

No Services shall commence until a SOW has been executed by both parties.

### 2.3 Client Responsibilities

Client shall:

- Provide timely access to systems, data, and personnel required to perform Services
- Communicate requirements clearly and promptly
- Designate a primary point of contact for the engagement
- Respond to Agency requests for clarification or approval within 2 business days
- Be responsible for backup and security of client-controlled data

## 3. FEES AND PAYMENT TERMS

### 3.1 Fee Structure

Unless otherwise specified in a SOW, fees are calculated as follows:

- **Hourly Rate:** [RATE] per hour (billed in 0.5-hour increments)
- **Fixed Price Projects:** As specified in individual SOW

### 3.2 Payment Terms - Standard

- **Payment Due:** Net 15 days from invoice date
- **Invoice Frequency:** Monthly in arrears, or as specified in SOW
- **Currency:** USD

### 3.3 Payment Terms - Large Projects

For projects with estimated fees exceeding $2,000 USD:

- **Upfront Deposit:** 50% of total estimated project fee due upon SOW execution
- **Final Payment:** Remaining 50% due upon delivery of Deliverables
- **Ongoing Retainers:** Billed monthly in advance

### 3.4 Late Payment

- Invoices unpaid after 30 days are considered past due
- Late payments accrue interest at 1.5% per month or the maximum rate permitted by law, whichever is lower
- Agency may suspend Services upon 10 days' written notice if payment is 30+ days overdue

### 3.5 Expenses

Unless otherwise specified in the SOW:

- Agency does not charge additional fees for hosting, software subscriptions, or API costs
- These costs are included in quoted fees
- Any significant, unplanned expenses will be communicated to Client before incurring

### 3.6 Scope Creep and Change Requests

- Changes to scope, deliverables, or timeline beyond the SOW constitute change requests
- Change requests require a written amendment signed by both parties
- Work performed on out-of-scope items will be invoiced at the applicable hourly rate

## 6. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 6.1 Definition

Confidential Information includes all non-public information disclosed by either party, including:

- Client's business plans, data, systems, and operations
- Agency's methodologies, tools, client lists, and pricing
- Information marked "Confidential"
- Information reasonably understood to be confidential in context

### 6.2 Obligations

Each party agrees to:

- Maintain Confidential Information in strict confidence
- Limit access to employees and contractors with a legitimate need to know
- Protect Confidential Information using the same degree of care used for their own (no less than reasonable care)
- Use Confidential Information solely for performing or receiving Services under this Agreement

### 6.3 Exclusions

Confidentiality obligations do not apply to information that:

- Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement
- Is rightfully received by the recipient from a third party without confidentiality restrictions
- Was independently developed without reference to the disclosing party's Confidential Information
- Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, or court order (with notice to allow protective measures)

### 6.4 Return of Confidential Information

Upon termination or Client request, Agency shall:

- Return or destroy (at Client's option) all Client Confidential Information in its possession
- Certify in writing that such return or destruction is complete
- Retain one archival copy as required by law or for legal defense

### 6.5 Duration

Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for [3 YEARS] or as required by law.

## 9. TERMINATION

### 9.1 Termination for Convenience

Either party may terminate this Agreement with written notice, provided:

- **Minimum Notice Period:** 30 days' written notice
- **Effective Date:** Termination takes effect on the date specified in the notice (or 30 days after notice if no date specified)

### 9.2 Termination for Cause

Either party may terminate immediately without notice if:

- The other party materially breaches this Agreement and fails to cure within 15 days of written notice
- The other party becomes insolvent, files bankruptcy, or ceases business operations
- As permitted by law

### 9.3 Effect of Termination

Upon termination:

- Agency shall immediately cease work, except as necessary to wind down
- Client shall pay for all Services rendered through the termination date, including:
  - Work completed and accepted by Client
  - Unbilled time and expenses incurred through the notice period
  - Any upfront deposits credited against final amounts owed
- Agency shall deliver all work-in-progress and materials upon Client's request
- Sections 4 (IP), 6 (Confidentiality), 7 (Limitation of Liability), 8 (Warranties), 10 (Indemnification), 11 (Governing Law), and this Section 9.3 survive termination

### 9.4 Early Termination by Client

If Client terminates without cause before the term specified in the SOW:

- Client remains responsible for payment for all completed work and accepted deliverables
- Client may be responsible for non-refundable third-party costs incurred on its behalf
- Agency shall make best efforts to mitigate third-party costs upon receiving termination notice

### 9.5 Survival

Provisions regarding Confidentiality, IP ownership, Limitation of Liability, Indemnification, and Governing Law survive any termination of this Agreement.

## 10. INDEMNIFICATION

### 10.1 Agency Indemnification

Agency shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Client from any third-party claims that:

- Deliverables infringe any U.S. patent, copyright, or trade secret of a third party
- Agency's breach of this Agreement causes direct harm to Client

**Conditions:**

- Client must promptly notify Agency in writing of the claim
- Client shall not settle the claim without Agency's prior written consent
- Agency shall control the defense, including legal counsel selection

### 10.2 Client Indemnification

Client shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless Agency from any third-party claims that:

- Client-provided materials, data, or specifications infringe third-party IP rights
- Client's use of Deliverables beyond the scope authorized in this Agreement violates third-party rights
- Client's implementation or modification of Deliverables causes infringement

### 10.3 Indemnification Procedures

The indemnified party shall:

- Provide prompt written notice of the claim
- Cooperate reasonably in the defense at the indemnifying party's expense
- Grant the indemnifying party sole control of defense and settlement

### 10.4 Indemnification Remedies

If Deliverables are held to infringe, Agency may, at its option and expense:

- Obtain the right for Client to continue using the Deliverables
- Modify the Deliverables to be non-infringing
- If neither option is practicable, terminate the SOW and refund fees paid for the infringing Deliverable
